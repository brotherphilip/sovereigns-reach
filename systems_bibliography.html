Overview & Architecture

Two linked play spaces: a tactical City View (build and run one settlement) and a strategic World Map (rule a kingdom of cities). Engine: Godot 4.6.3, GDScript.

  • Deterministic simulation core driven by a fixed-tick SimulationClock (20 ticks/s; 240 ticks = one game-day = 12 s at Normal speed). simulation/core/SimulationClock.gd
  • GameState autoload holds the world, players, AI factions, citizens; simulate_tick(t) advances everything each tick. simulation/core/GameState.gd
  • Player actions route through a CommandQueue (drained inside the tick) for determinism; a few view-only/clock-independent actions call GameState directly (e.g. speed, world-map orders).
  • Pure-data systems over shared dictionaries; the view layer (view/) only renders.

The build goal: a human can start a new game and play an engaged 20-minute life — survive, understand what's happening, and never hit a wall. (Verified end-to-end via the change.md playtest loop.)

Time & Calendar sun-aligned calendar day (2026-06-18)

simulation/world/SeasonSystem.gd, simulation/core/SimulationClock.gd

Three nested clocks

  • Tick — 20/s, the simulation heartbeat.
  • Economic day — TICKS_PER_GAME_DAY = 240 (12s). The internal cadence for food consumption, AI upkeep, garrison regen, prestige. Balance is tuned to it and it is kept fixed.
  • Calendar day — TICKS_PER_CALENDAR_DAY = 3600 (≈5 calendar days per sunrise). This is what the player SEES and what milestones/objectives/victory are keyed to, so the day counter lines up with the sun instead of racing to ~75/sunrise. SimulationClock.calendar_day().

Because only the calendar layer was rescaled (the economy still runs on the 240-tick day), difficulty per real-minute is unchanged — only the day NUMBER. Key beats: "A Sovereign's Reign" milestone = Day 12; reign beats Day 6/9; objectives Day 6/12; the King's Peace (AI siege + tribute grace) now lasts 10 sun cycles (PLAYER_GRACE_DAYS 750 economic days ≈ calendar Day 50); the defence warning ~Day 3. Realm events are paced to one every 3–5 sun cycles (was ~one per cycle).

Day / night cycle (the sun)

  • A full day↔night spans DAY_NIGHT_TICKS = 18000 ≈ 15 real minutes at Normal (~3 min at Fastest), opening at noon.
  • night_factor(tick) 0 (noon) … 1 (midnight); a power curve (NIGHT_SKEW 1.8) keeps night the shorter ~⅓ (≈10 min day, ≈5 min night). is_night(), phase_name() → Day/Dusk/Night/Dawn (shown in the HUD).
  • Decoupled from the 240-tick economy game-day so the village runs lively while the sky moves slowly.

Seasonal calendar (keyed to the day/night cycle)

  • A "day" = one day/night. SKY_DAYS_PER_SEASON = 2 → an 8-day year (~2 hours); seasons advance at the same scale as the year (no more flickering within a day).
  • season_at_tick(tick) drives harvest gating, weather, terrain/foliage tints, seasonal events.
  • Spring/Summer/Autumn/Winter affect crop yields, snow (winter-only), and visuals.

Economy — physical hauling

simulation/economy/ResourceTick.gd, StorageSystem.gd · simulation/world/CitizenSystem.gd

  • Hauling chain: a worker physically walks to a resource node, gathers, carries to its building, processes, and delivers to a stockpile/granary — output is credited only on delivery.
  • Storage caps: production stops when storage is full; build stockpiles/granaries to store more.
  • Food: the no-tech apple_orchard is the staple. Harvest gating gives a bumper crop in-season and an 0.85× year-round trickle off-season (raised from 0.6 when seasons were lengthened, so the staple feeds the village even across a long off-season). Granary stores food; population eats daily.
  • Chains: woodcutter→stockpile, quarry, iron mine, wheat→mill→bakery (bread), hops→brewery (ale), pig/dairy farms (meat/cheese/leather), trading post (gold).
  • Weather & shire biome modifiers scale farm/mine output; drought/snow can zero crop yields.

People & Citizens day/night behaviour iter 38

simulation/world/PeopleSystem.gd, CitizenSystem.gd

  • Living villagers with life-stages (baby→child→adult→midlife→elder), families, genetics (inherited skin/hair), births gated on housing rooms. Aging keys off its own scale (48 game-days = 1 life-year), independent of the seasonal calendar. Each wears a per-person tunic from a muted peasant palette (id-indexed) so a crowd reads as varied folk, not one uniform cohort.
  • Only working-age adults take jobs; CitizenSystem pulls idle villagers into job-workers (woodcutter/reaper/smith…) that walk to buildings and toil, and into builders for unfinished sites.
  • At night the town goes home to sleep: every villager walks to their allotted house (round-robin over hovels/hall), to the door, and steps INSIDE (not drawn). Workers are released home too, keeping only a 1-worker skeleton crew on essential FOOD buildings so the larder still trickles overnight; the day reconcile re-staffs everyone at dawn — so the streets visibly empty after dark and refill by day. (Opt-in day_night flag; the real game enables it. Proven by tests/TestNight.gd + tools/ProbeHaulEconomy.gd: midnight 8/12 asleep, 4 on the night shift.)
  • Construction is pure builder labour (no materials needed); builders come only from idle villagers. AI towns reserve builders when works are pending; the player is warned if every villager is employed and works stall.

Buildings & Construction

simulation/buildings/BuildingRegistry.gd, BuildingState.gd

  • Categories: Civic (Village Hall, Hovel, Market, Apothecary, Well…), Harvesting, Food, Military, Defense (palisade, stone wall, gatehouse, towers).
  • A fresh game starts with no buildings — the player must place a Village Hall first (onboarding tutorial guides this). Freshly-finished workplaces auto-staff to max.
  • Buildable road paths (built over time, speed up movement); minimum spacing between buildings.
  • Fire can ignite buildings (weather/risk); apothecary/well provide sanitation; disease scales with crowding.

Popularity & Survival

simulation/economy/PopularityEngine.gd

  • Daily popularity delta = food ration + variety + ale (×inn coverage) + religion + tax + events, smoothed ×0.05/day. Loss conditions: keep/hall destroyed, popularity < 10 (revolt), or starvation → collapse.
  • Siege morale is defense-aware: an unready realm panics under a looming siege (active_siege −8/day) while a prepared one (walls/towers + garrison) holds (active_siege_defended −3/day). The siege telegraph names the kingdom, gives the ETA in days, and tells you if your defences are steadying the people.
  • A one-time restless-people warning fires when popularity drifts low, pointing at the morale levers (Village Feast edict, lower taxes, church/inn).
  • The day-boundary also warns when construction has stalled for lack of free builders.
  • A one-time low-food warning fires when the granary drops below ~3 days' need (scaled by population + ration) — re-arming after it recovers — so a drought-driven famine is never silent until starvation (food 0). Points at the levers: lower the food Ration, the Frugal Tables edict, or more Orchards/Farms.

Military & Combat

simulation/units/*, simulation/combat/CombatSystem.gd, simulation/ai/*

  • 20 unit types (peasants→militia→pikemen/swordsmen/archers/crossbows, captains, siege engines). Recruit at a Barracks; units muster at the campfire and take orders.
  • Tactical combat: 8-direction A* pathfinding, arrows as projectiles, siege engines, auto-aggro defense. Friend-or-foe shown via blue (yours) / red (enemy) discs under each unit.
  • King's Peace: a long calm establishment — rival factions can't siege OR send tribute/ransom demands to a freshly-arrived realm for its first PLAYER_GRACE_DAYS = 750 economic days (= 10 sun cycles); a siege then assembles (telegraphed) before it can fell the keep.
  • No startup garrison (2026-06-18): every realm — AI and player alike — begins with an EMPTY garrison (the generator's random troop_count is ignored at start) and must raise its troops over time; KingdomEconomy regenerates a settled holding's militia toward its cap day by day.
  • AI factions (incl. The Ashen Barony) grow economies, recruit, demand tribute (payable in gold/iron), and march.

Strategic Layer — the World Map hex-map overhaul (2026-06-18)

simulation/strategic/*, view/worldmap/*

Hand-drawn hex look: the continent now renders as shaded HEXAGON tiles with a faint lattice and layered terrain relief (tree copses, boulders, dry-land shrubs, snow-capped peaks, shaded shoreline shallows); faction territory reads as a crisp coloured BORDER rather than a muddy fill, so the biomes show through. Great houses + the player all start undeveloped (development 0) and build their realms up over time. view/worldmap/WorldMapView.gd.

A procedural biome continent of cities held by rival kingdoms (incl. the player's "Emerald March"). Kingdoms grow, develop cities, raise armies, wage campaigns, and conduct diplomacy each strategic day — and the player can do all of it too, via the world-map UI:

  • Right-click a city to select it (cyan ring); left-click enters/rules it.
  • ⚒ Develop a selected own city (spends treasury/wood/stone, raises its development).
  • ⚔ Raise Army at a selected own city (levies soldiers for gold).
  • ⚔ March a city's army → right-click an enemy target to launch a campaign (BFS road path).
  • 🕊 Diplomacy — offer a truce to / declare war on a rival kingdom; a sworn truce is honoured by AI targeting (they keep off your lands).
  • Realm stores (treasury/wood/stone/cities) shown on the map so investment is plannable.
  • The realm's strategic economy advances even while you play your city, so these actions stay fundable.
  • Seat-shield: your actively-ruled seat city can't be taken by an abstract strategic assault — it falls only through a tactical siege that fells its keep. Peripheral cities are still at stake.

Entering a rival city spectates a generated town sized to its development (CityGenerator); it grows buildings as its development rises.

World Events & Decisions

simulation/world/WorldEventSystem.gd

  • 47 data-driven daily events (bounded, positive-leaning): merchants, foraging, weddings, a wanderer joining, wolves, theft… plus seasonal ones (Spring Fair, Long Summer Days, Harvest Home, Harvest Moon, Hearth Tales, First Snow, Deep Frost) gated to their season, and night-flavoured beats (A Starlit Night).
  • Choice events (a popup that pauses time): a baron's loan, brigand toll, refugees at the gate, a knight errant, poachers, a grand tourney, a marriage alliance, a neighbour's plea — each with real trade-offs applied via a command.
  • Effects are clamped (never below 0 / never instant-revolt); content compounds by adding dict entries.
  • Fully voiced (iter 79): all 41 events have a "grim war herald" VO (audio/narration/event_<id>.wav, raw Chatterbox, no FX) that NarrationPlayer speaks the moment the pop-up appears — the realm narrates its own life.

Tech & Edicts

simulation/tech/*, simulation/edicts/EdictSystem.gd

  • Technology tree (Agriculture/Industry/Military/Statecraft) — research with prestige to unlock buildings (e.g. Crop Tiers → Wheat/Hops farms). Research fires a readable "unlocked X" notice.
  • Royal Edicts — decrees with edict-point costs/cooldowns. Two are reachable with no tech from the start: Village Feast (+6 popularity, a morale burst) and Frugal Tables (−8% food, a standing survival lever); the rest unlock via Statecraft. Edict notices read the decree's name, not its raw id.

Audio & Music music iter 164

simulation/audio/* (autoloads: AudioManager, NarrationPlayer, MusicPlayer)

  • SFX (AudioManager.gd + SfxGen.gd): event sounds are synthesized at runtime (zero asset files) and played off real EventBus signals (build/demolish, combat hit/death, siege incoming, edict, prestige jump, low-popularity), each with per-event gain + a min-gap throttle so flurries don't machine-gun. Every UI button is auto-wired to a soft click via the scene tree.
  • Narration (NarrationPlayer.gd, see also the narration-voiceover memory): a "grim herald" VO speaks every pop-up; clips are dropped in as audio/narration/<key>.wav and loaded from raw bytes at runtime (no editor import).
  • Background music (MusicPlayer.gd, iter 164): an auto-updating playlist — drop any .mp3/.ogg/.wav into audio/Music/ and it plays with NO editor reimport (each track is byte-loaded at runtime); the folder is re-scanned whenever the playlist wraps, so songs added mid-session join on the next loop. All music routes through a dedicated "Music" audio bus built at runtime carrying a "slightly distant / lower-fidelity / older" treatment: high-pass (220 Hz) + low-pass (3000 Hz) for a muffled small-speaker feel, a subtle LoFi bitcrush for old-record grain, and a light reverb for a sense of space. The bus sits at −13 dB so the music underscores rather than overwhelms (tunable; set_music_volume_db() is ready for an options slider). Ducking (iter 165): while the herald narration speaks, the music bed smoothly glides down ~11 dB and back up after, so the voice always reads clearly over the music (NarrationPlayer.is_speaking() polled per-frame; glide is pause-proof). Audio mixer (iter 166-167): the pause menu carries Master / Music / SFX volume sliders, each persisted across sessions (user://settings.cfg via ConfigFile; slider floor = Off/mute). SFX route to their own bus (separate from Music + Master), so the player tunes the whole mix to taste — the "not overwhelming" requirement, in their hands. Guarded by tests/TestMusic.gd (20/0).
