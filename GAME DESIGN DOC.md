1. The Concept
1.1 Emergent PvE Mechanics

1.1.1 AI Warlords: (1) Factions hold regions. (2) AI simulates a real economy. (3) AI uses settler carts. (4) Border proximity causes war. (5) Warlords can be killed.
1.1.2 Roaming Bandits: (1) Bandits patrol the map. (2) They target trade carts. (3) Bandits lack castles. (4) Camps spawn in forests. (5) Defeating them yields loot.
1.1.3 Weather Events: (1) Weather alters conditions. (2) Rain slows movement. (3) Drought kills wheat. (4) Snow drains food. (5) Fog hides army UI.
1.1.4 Telegraphed Sieges: (1) Attacks are visible early. (2) Assembly takes up to 48h. (3) UI reveals troop counts. (4) Players can reinforce. (5) Tents can be ambushed.
1.1.5 Difficulty Scaling: (1) Danger scales with server age. (2) Early game tests space. (3) Mid-game tests walls. (4) Late-game tests siege skills. (5) AI gets passive buffs.  
1.2 Regional Capitals

1.2.1 Shire Divisions: (1) Map is split into Shires. (2) Each holds one Capital. (3) Borders dictate taxes. (4) Shires have biome traits. (5) Capture flips map color.
1.2.2 Capital Upgrades: (1) Friends fund the Capital. (2) Requires massive resources. (3) Upgrades are permanent. (4) Multiple upgrade paths exist. (5) Grand Surveyors expand borders.
1.2.3 Passive Buffs: (1) Buffs affect the whole server. (2) Stone quarrying speeds up. (3) Crop yields increase. (4) Troop recruitment quickens. (5) UI displays active buffs.
1.2.4 Resource Donation: (1) Requires localized storage. (2) Carts haul goods to center. (3) Donation tracks per player. (4) Creates resource sinks. (5) Prevents endgame hoarding.
1.2.5 Cooperative Ambition: (1) Replaces PvP voting. (2) Unites the player group. (3) Acts as a shared goal. (4) Cannot be destroyed by AI. (5) Dictates regional control.  
1.3 Specialized Co-op

1.3.1 Agricultural Roles: (1) Players settle fertile valleys. (2) Focus on wheat and ale. (3) Supplies server food. (4) Requires open space. (5) Highly vulnerable to fire.
1.3.2 Industrial Roles: (1) Settle mountain terrain. (2) Forge weapons and iron. (3) Arm the server. (4) Requires dense housing. (5) Safe from cavalry.
1.3.3 Trade Carts: (1) Automate supply lines. (2) Move raw materials. (3) Move luxury goods. (4) Vulnerable to interception. (5) Speed relies on roads.
1.3.4 Server Supply Chains: (1) Connects specialized bases. (2) One player mines, one crafts. (3) Requires logistical planning. (4) Creates interdependency. (5) Alleviates grid congestion.
1.3.5 Base Interdependence: (1) Players share excess goods. (2) Bypasses individual bottlenecks. (3) Allows extreme specialization. (4) Fosters cooperative defense. (5) Scales server power exponentially.  
1.4 Non-PvP Adjustments

1.4.1 Elimination of Raiding: (1) Players cannot burn allies. (2) Offline protection is innate. (3) Focus shifts to AI. (4) Reduces player toxicity. (5) Encourages beautiful castle designs.
1.4.2 Shared Vision: (1) Allies share fog of war. (2) Spy data is communal. (3) Scout data is permanent. (4) Eases coordination. (5) Reveals AI troop movements.
1.4.3 Cooperative Milestones: (1) Progress is shared. (2) Unlocks group Edicts. (3) Triggers server events. (4) Grants global prestige. (5) Paces the endgame.
1.4.4 Friendly Fire Rules: (1) Catapults do not hurt allies. (2) Pitch fires ignore friendly troops. (3) Moats can be crossed by allies. (4) Prevents griefing. (5) Simplifies shared sieges.
1.4.5 Asynchronous Support: (1) Players log in at different times. (2) Can send passive resources. (3) Can garrison troops in ally bases. (4) Trade routes run offline. (5) Server persists 24/7.  
1.5 Session & Server Persistence

1.5.1 Self-Hosted Architecture: (1) Players host the world. (2) Persistent environment. (3) Password protected. (4) Customizable tick rates. (5) Low latency for friends.
1.5.2 Real-Time Progression: (1) Research takes real time. (2) Sieges setup over hours. (3) Travel takes real time. (4) Allows daily check-ins. (5) Rewards long-term planning.
1.5.3 Offline Simulation: (1) Economy runs while offline. (2) Granaries auto-distribute food. (3) Production queues process. (4) AI movements continue. (5) Stockpiles can fill up.
1.5.4 Save States: (1) Server auto-saves hourly. (2) Allows manual rollbacks. (3) Prevents data loss. (4) Protects against bugs. (5) Can export map seeds.
1.5.5 Player Scaling: (1) Supports small friend groups. (2) Scales AI bases accordingly. (3) Adjusts resource nodes. (4) Modifies capital costs. (5) Keeps economy tight.

3. The Core Loop: The Popularity Engine

Calculation: P=ΔF+ΔA+ΔR−T±E

  
3.1 Food Variables (ΔF)

3.1.1 Ration Quantities: (1) Ranges from zero to quadruple. (2) Higher rations boost popularity. (3) Zero rations cause starvation. (4) Depletes granaries faster. (5) Requires constant monitoring.
3.1.2 Food Variety: (1) Multiple food types stack. (2) Apples provide base sustenance. (3) Cheese adds small bonuses. (4) Meat adds medium bonuses. (5) Bread provides maximum bonuses.
3.1.3 Distribution Speed: (1) Granaries hand out food. (2) Proximity to Keep matters. (3) Multiple granaries increase speed. (4) Empty granaries trigger warnings. (5) Spoilage reduces totals.
3.1.4 Agricultural Space: (1) Farms require green terrain. (2) Space is highly limited. (3) Forces spatial puzzles. (4) Dictates food caps. (5) Encourages trade routes.

3.1.BAL Balance (ResourceTick, tuned via playthrough iter 159): peasants eat 0.5 food/day each (× ration multiplier). The no-tech Apple Orchard is the survival staple — interval 150 ticks, 3 apples/cycle/worker — so ~3 staffed orchards sustain a 50-soul village while the player banks prestige for crop_tiers (the bread chain: wheat_farm 360t×3, mill, bakery). Starting stock is 100 apples (≈ a few days' buffer to build the first orchards).
3.1.5 Peasant Diet Logic: (1) Peasants eat all available types. (2) Cannot restrict to one food. (3) Variety unlocks Prestige. (4) Famines plummet popularity. (5) Requires Edicts to fix quickly.  
3.2 Ale Distribution (ΔA)

3.2.1 Inn Coverage: (1) Inns distribute Ale. (2) Area of effect applies. (3) Multiple Inns stack coverage. (4) Costs wood to build. (5) Requires workers to run.
3.2.2 Ale Production: (1) Requires Hops Farms. (2) Breweries process Hops. (3) Ale is stored in Stockpiles. (4) Slow production rate. (5) Offsets high taxation.
3.2.3 Consumption Rates: (1) Players set Ale rations. (2) High rations drain stocks fast. (3) Max rations give huge popularity. (4) Zero rations give negative. (5) Highly space intensive.
3.2.4 Trade Value: (1) Ale sells for high gold. (2) Often demanded as tribute. (3) Good for automated carts. (4) AI prioritizes buying it. (5) Used in diplomatic bribes.
3.2.5 Morale Buffer: (1) Ale prevents immediate collapse. (2) Masks food shortages. (3) Stabilizes village during sieges. (4) Crucial for mid-game economy. (5) Subject to Royal Edicts.  
3.3 Religion Coverage (ΔR)

3.3.1 Church Placement: (1) Distributes Faith. (2) Has a localized radius. (3) Costs stone to build. (4) Requires priests. (5) Generates small popularity.
3.3.2 Cathedral Placement: (1) Massive area of effect. (2) Costs immense stone and gold. (3) Generates massive popularity. (4) Acts as a status symbol. (5) Immune to fire.
3.3.3 Marriage Events: (1) Churches randomly host weddings. (2) Gives temporary popularity spike. (3) Pauses worker for a minute. (4) Adds to overall Prestige. (5) Visible animation plays.
3.3.4 Blessing Rates: (1) Priests walk the streets. (2) Bless individual peasants. (3) Temporarily boosts worker speed. (4) Distance from church matters. (5) Monks can also bless.
3.3.5 Tech Prerequisites: (1) Religion is locked early. (2) Requires Statecraft research. (3) Upgrades increase radius. (4) Upgrades reduce stone cost. (5) Vital for high tax builds.  

3.3.IMPL Implemented (ReligionSystem.gd / GameState / PopularityEngine):
- Coverage (ΔR): sum(church/cathedral radius ÷ 4) ÷ hovel count, clamped 0–1, ×10 popularity; updated every tick. Church radius 12, cathedral 30. Locked behind the `monastic_orders` tech.
- Faith resource: active churches (+3/day), cathedrals (+8/day, scaled 0.5–1.0 by staffing) and each living Monk (+2/day) accrue Faith, scaled by coverage, up to a cap (church 30, cathedral 80). Stored in player.faith / faith_cap.
- Blessing: when Faith reaches 40 it is spent automatically, firing a +6 popularity event and a 3-game-day window of divine fire protection (−50% ignition risk). Emits EventBus.blessing_bestowed; exposed to the HUD as faith / faith_cap / blessing_active.
- Marriage events (3.3.3): implemented as random church popularity spikes (+4) scaling with coverage above 30%.
- Monk (3.3.4 "Monks can also bless"): now contributes Faith; street-walking/per-peasant blessing and radius/cost tech upgrades remain future work.

3.4 Taxation Variables (T)

3.4.1 Gold Extraction: (1) Taxes pull gold from peasants. (2) Positive taxes hurt popularity. (3) Negative taxes (bribes) boost it. (4) Zero taxes are neutral. (5) Vault holds the gold.
3.4.2 Military Funding: (1) Gold is needed for troops. (2) Weapons alone are not enough. (3) Captains cost massive gold. (4) Siege engines cost gold. (5) Taxes fuel the war.
3.4.3 Research Funding: (1) Tech tree requires gold. (2) Late tier tech is expensive. (3) Forces aggressive taxing. (4) Slows down early expansion. (5) Market sales supplement taxes.
3.4.4 Bribe Mechanics: (1) Handing out gold buys love. (2) Drains vault rapidly. (3) Used in emergencies. (4) Fixes sudden popularity drops. (5) Unsustainable long-term.
3.4.5 Tax Sliders: (1) UI provides a tax slider. (2) Updates popularity formula instantly. (3) Allows micro-adjustments. (4) Taxes tick daily. (5) Balances against Ale and Food.  
3.5 External Events (E)

3.5.1 Active Sieges: (1) Being attacked drops popularity. (2) Simulates peasant fear. (3) Requires morale buffers to survive. (4) Ends when siege ends. (5) Can cause mid-battle desertion.
3.5.2 Royal Edicts: (1) Edicts modify popularity. (2) Levy summons reduce it heavily. (3) Festivals boost it temporarily. (4) Agrarian subsidies offset food loss. (5) Tooltips explain math changes.
3.5.3 Disease Outbreaks: (1) Strikes crowded housing. (2) Plummets popularity. (3) Kills peasants over time. (4) Cured by Apothecaries. (5) Spreads if unmanaged.

3.5.3.IMPL Implemented (DiseaseSystem.gd / GameState / HUD):
- Public Health: a 0–100 score, base 40 + 60×sanitation, −15 in snow, −15 when food variety < 2. Sanitation = apothecary coverage + ½×well coverage (each covers ~6 hovels; apothecaries need a worker, wells do not). Stored in player.health.
- New building `Well` (Civic, cheap, unstaffed) — a passive sanitation source alongside the Apothecary (active cure).
- Outbreak: only when crowded (≥5 hovels) and sanitation < 0.5; daily chance = 0.12 × (1 − health/100), so healthier realms are far safer.
- Severity model: disease is graded 0–100, not binary. It spreads +15/day×(1−sanitation) and is cured −30/day×apothecary coverage; deaths/day = ceil(pop × severity% × 0.04) (so severe plagues kill faster) and it ends when severity hits 0. player.disease_active mirrors severity>0; the −10 "disease_outbreak" popularity event fires while active.
- HUD shows Health, flipping to a red "Plague! n%" severity readout during an outbreak.

3.5.4 AI Demands: (1) AI sends envoys for tribute. (2) Refusing drops popularity slightly. (3) Accepting costs resources. (4) Triggers embargoes. (5) Precedes military action.
3.5.5 Weather Penalties: (1) Blizzards drop popularity. (2) Heat waves drop popularity. (3) Forces ration increases to compensate. (4) Adds unpredictability. (5) Requires stockpiled reserves.

4.1.4 Decay Rates: (1) Losing villages drops prestige. (2) Starving peasants stalls it. (3) Defeats remove chunks. (4) Forces consistent management. (5) Prevents afk farming.

- Faith resource: active churches (+3/day), cathedrals (+8/day, scaled 0.5–1.0 by staffing) and each living Monk (+2/day) accrue Faith, scaled by coverage, up to a cap (church 30, cathedral 80). Stored in player.faith / faith_cap.
- Blessing: when Faith reaches 40 it is spent automatically, firing a +6 popularity event and a 3-game-day window of divine fire protection (−50% ignition risk). Emits EventBus.blessing_bestowed; exposed to the HUD as faith / faith_cap / blessing_active.
- Marriage events (3.3.3): implemented as random church popularity spikes (+4) scaling with coverage above 30%.
- Monk (3.3.4 "Monks can also bless"): now contributes Faith; street-walking/per-peasant blessing and radius/cost tech upgrades remain future work.

3.5.3.IMPL Implemented (DiseaseSystem.gd / GameState / HUD):
- Public Health: a 0–100 score, base 40 + 60×sanitation, −15 in snow, −15 when food variety < 2. Sanitation = apothecary coverage + ½×well coverage (each covers ~6 hovels; apothecaries need a worker, wells do not). Stored in player.health.
- New building `Well` (Civic, cheap, unstaffed) — a passive sanitation source alongside the Apothecary (active cure).
- Outbreak: only when crowded (≥5 hovels) and sanitation < 0.5; daily chance = 0.12 × (1 − health/100), so healthier realms are far safer.
- Severity model: disease is graded 0–100, not binary. It spreads +15/day×(1−sanitation) and is cured −30/day×apothecary coverage; deaths/day = ceil(pop × severity% × 0.04) (so severe plagues kill faster) and it ends when severity hits 0. player.disease_active mirrors severity>0; the −10 "disease_outbreak" popularity event fires while active.
- HUD shows Health, flipping to a red "Plague! n%" severity readout during an outbreak.

8.5.3 Espionage Scouting: (1) Scouting reveals AI blueprints. (2) Shows wall layouts. (3) Identifies weak points. (4) Vital before declaring war. (5) Prepares the co-op team.
8.5.4 Coordinated Sieges: (1) Persistent world requires 12 hours to setup. (2) Players time battles for weekends. (3) Multiple players attack one castle. (4) Focus catapult fire on breaches. (5) Target the Keep directly.
8.5.5 Vassalization: (1) Capturing a Capital ends the faction. (2) Vassalizes remaining farming villages. (3) Routes daily tithes to players. (4) Flips region colors. (5) Eliminates the AI completely.
